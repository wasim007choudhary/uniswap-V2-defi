---

# 💡 The Problem

`2x` depends on which asset we decide to call **Token X**.

`2y` depends on which asset we decide to call **Token Y**.

The liquidity function itself changes depending on our choice of units.

Ideally...

Liquidity should not care.

---

# 🧠 Introducing Symmetry

This motivates an important mathematical property.

A liquidity function should satisfy:

```text
F(x, y)

=

F(y, x)
```

This means:

If we swap the order of the two tokens...

Nothing should fundamentally change.

The liquidity of the pool should remain the same.

---

# Testing Our Candidates

## Candidate

```text
F(x, y)

=

2x
```

Swap the variables.

```text

=

2y
```

Generally:

```text
2x

≠

2y
```

❌ Not symmetric.

---

## Candidate

```text
F(x, y)

=

2y
```

Swap.

Again:

```text
2y

≠

2x
```

❌ Not symmetric.

# Another Candidate

✅ Perfectly symmetric.

# ✅ Clarification

Symmetry alone is **not enough**.

It is only **one** property we want.


But that doesn't automatically make it a good liquidity function.
