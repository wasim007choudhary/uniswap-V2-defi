# 9. Uniswap LP Share Formula

# Part 2 — Router vs Pair, Direct Donations, and Why `Math.min()` Still Exists

---

# 📌 The Next Question

At the end of Part 1 we concluded:

```text
The Router returns extra tokens.
```

Immediately another question arose.

> **If the Router already guarantees the correct ratio... why does `Pair.mint()` still calculate `Math.min()`?**

Wouldn't that be redundant?

---

# 🧠 The Answer

The Pair contract follows one simple philosophy.

> **Trust nobody.**

Not even the Router.

---

# Why?

Because ERC20 tokens can always be transferred directly.

Someone can completely bypass the Router.

For example:

```solidity
token0.transfer(pair, amount0);
token1.transfer(pair, amount1);

pair.mint(to);
```

No Router involved.

The Pair has absolutely no guarantee that:

- the correct ratio was used,
- the Router was called,
- or even that the sender behaved honestly.

---

# Example

Current pool:

```text
100 ETH

100,000 USDC
```

Bob ignores the Router.

He transfers:

```text
10 ETH

15,000 USDC
```

directly into the Pair.

The Pair simply observes:

```text
amount0 = 10 ETH

amount1 = 15,000 USDC
```

It has no context.

It only knows:

> "These tokens arrived."

---

# Should The Pair Trust Bob?

Absolutely not.

Instead it independently computes:

```text
Ownership implied by ETH

↓

10%
```

and

```text
Ownership implied by USDC

↓

15%
```

Then safely mints LP tokens using:

```solidity
Math.min(...)
```

Result:

```text
10%
```

ownership.

Exactly fair.

---

# 💬 Our Discussion

At this point you asked:

> **"What happens to the extra 5,000 USDC?"**

Initially it sounds like it should simply disappear.

It doesn't.

---

# The Extra Is NOT Returned

Unlike the Router...

The Pair cannot return tokens.

The tokens are already inside the contract.

So after Bob transfers:

```text
10 ETH

15,000 USDC
```

the Pair physically contains:

```text
110 ETH

115,000 USDC
```

The extra USDC stays inside the pool.

---

# 💬 Another Important Discussion

You then asked:

> **"So the extra didn't get added to the pool but distributed to the holders?"**

A small correction was needed.

The more accurate statement is:

> **The extra remains inside the pool.**

Nothing is physically distributed.

Instead...

Because LP tokens represent ownership of the entire pool...

Every LP automatically benefits from the increased pool value.

---

# Example

Suppose before Bob arrived:

```text
Alice

50%

Charlie

50%
```

Bob now receives:

```text
10%
```

ownership.

Approximately:

```text
Alice

45%
```

```text
Charlie

45%
```

```text
Bob

10%
```

The donated:

```text
5,000 USDC
```

is still inside the pool.

Its value is simply shared according to LP ownership.

Nobody receives tokens directly.

Everyone's LP tokens simply become worth more.

---

# 🧠 Better Wording

Instead of saying:

> **"The extra gets distributed."**

It is more accurate to say:

> **"The extra remains inside the pool, increasing the value of every LP token."**

---

# 💬 Another Excellent Question

You then asked:

> **"What about traders? Doesn't this increase the price inside the pool?"**

Excellent observation.

Yes.

Temporarily.

---

# Example

Suppose someone donates:

```text
0 ETH

5,000 USDC
```

Pool becomes:

```text
100 ETH

105,000 USDC
```

Pool price becomes:

```text
1050 USDC / ETH
```

instead of:

```text
1000 USDC / ETH
```

The price has increased.

---

# Does This Break Uniswap?

No.

Because Uniswap expects arbitrage.

Professional traders immediately notice:

```text
Uniswap

↓

1050
```

```text
Other Exchanges

↓

1000
```

They buy ETH elsewhere...

Then sell it into the Uniswap pool.

Eventually the reserves change until:

```text
Price

↓

1000 again.
```

The donation remains.

The incorrect price disappears.

---

# 🧠 Huge Realization

Donating tokens can temporarily move the pool price.

But arbitrage naturally restores the correct market price.

The donated value remains inside the pool.

Existing LPs benefit.

---

# Another Question

You then correctly said:

> **"If the Router had been used, those extra tokens wouldn't have been wasted."**

Exactly.

---

# Router Path

User wants:

```text
10 ETH

15,000 USDC
```

Router computes:

```text
Optimal

↓

10 ETH

10,000 USDC
```

Only those amounts are transferred.

The remaining:

```text
5,000 USDC
```

never leaves the user's wallet.

Nothing is wasted.

---

# Pair Path

If someone bypasses the Router:

```text
10 ETH

15,000 USDC
```

already arrives inside the Pair.

The Pair cannot return tokens.

It simply mints fair LP tokens.

The extra becomes a donation.

---

# Separation Of Responsibilities

This led to another important realization.

Router and Pair have completely different responsibilities.

---

## Router

Responsible for:

- Reading reserves.
- Computing optimal ratios.
- Preventing overpayment.
- Returning unused tokens.
- Making UX smooth.

---

## Pair

Responsible for:

- Minting LP tokens fairly.
- Burning LP tokens.
- Updating reserves.
- Never trusting external callers.

---

# 🧠 Biggest Realization

The Router exists for convenience.

The Pair exists for correctness.

The Pair would still function correctly even if the Router did not exist.

The Router simply prevents users from making expensive mistakes.

---
