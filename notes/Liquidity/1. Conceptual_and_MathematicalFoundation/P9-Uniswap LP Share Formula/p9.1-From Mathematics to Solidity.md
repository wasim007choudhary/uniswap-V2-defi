# ЁЯТм Our Discussion

At this point we asked:

> **Should Alice receive LP Tokens for 15% ownership?**

No.

Because she didn't contribute enough ETH.

If she were given:

```text

```

ownership...

she would effectively receive ownership for:

```text

```

```text

```

That would dilute every existing LP.

---

# The Fair Solution

Ownership must always be limited by the asset that contributed the **least**.

Therefore:

```text
ETH

тЖУ

10%
```

```text
USDC

тЖУ

15%
```

LP Tokens must represent:

```text
10%
```

ownership.

---

# Solidity's Solution

Instead of trusting either calculation...

The Pair computes both.

```solidity
Math.min(
    amount0 * totalSupply / reserve0,
    amount1 * totalSupply / reserve1
)
```

It simply asks:

> **Which asset contributed the smaller ownership percentage?**

That percentage determines the LP Tokens to mint.

---

# ЁЯза Biggest Realization

`Math.min()` is **not** a mathematical trick.

It is a fairness rule.

It guarantees that LP ownership is determined by the limiting contribution.

Nobody can receive ownership for assets they never actually supplied.
