# 9. Uniswap LP Share Formula

# Part 3A — The First Liquidity Provider (Version 2)

---

# 🎯 Goal

Until now, every LP minting formula we derived assumed one thing:

> **The pool already exists.**

That means:

- The pool already has reserves.
- LP Tokens already exist.
- Someone already owns the pool.
- New LPs simply receive ownership proportional to what already exists.

But...

Someone has to create the pool in the first place.

This chapter answers:

> **How does Uniswap mint LP Tokens when there are no LP Tokens yet?**

---

Our final LP minting formula became:

```text
LP Tokens to Mint

=

(dx/x)

×

Total LP Supply
```

or equivalently

```text

=

(dy/y)

×

```

We even mapped it directly to Solidity.

```solidity

```

and


Everything matched perfectly.

Or so it seemed...

# Even Worse...

Suppose we somehow ignored the division.

We still have

```text
Anything

×

0

=

0
```

Meaning Alice would receive

```text
0 LP Tokens
```

Clearly impossible.

She owns the **entire pool**.

Receiving zero ownership makes no sense.

---

# 🧠 Biggest Realization

This is not a bug in our formula.

Our formula was never designed for this situation.

It assumes:

> **There is already an ownership system.**

But the first LP is the one **creating** the ownership system.

Nothing exists yet.

Therefore proportional minting cannot be used.

# 💬 Our Discussion

At this point we stopped and asked a much more fundamental question.

Forget formulas.

Forget Solidity.

Suppose Alice creates a brand-new pool.

How many LP Tokens should exist?

---

# 💬 Our Discussion

This led to one of the most interesting realizations in the chapter.

You noticed that...

All three answers actually work.

That feels strange.

How can:

```text
100

1000

1,000,000
```

all be correct?

---

# The Reason

Because LP Tokens are **not assets**.

They are **ownership receipts**.

If there is only one owner...

Any positive number of receipts still represents

```text
100%
```

ownership.

The absolute number doesn't matter.

Only the percentages matter.

---

# Company Analogy

Imagine creating a company.

You invest

```text
$1,000,000
```

Should the company issue

```text
100 Shares?
```

Sure.

---

Should it issue

```text
1000 Shares?
```

Also fine.

---

Should it issue

```text
1 Billion Shares?
```

Still fine.

The ownership doesn't change.

If you own every share...

You own

```text
100%
```

of the company.

Exactly the same idea applies to LP Tokens.

---

# 🧠 Huge Realization

LP Tokens are simply an accounting system.

They don't create value.

They only describe ownership.

Therefore...

The initial LP supply is **mathematically arbitrary**.

That was a very important realization from our discussion.

---

# 💬 Our Discussion

At this point you understood something subtle.

The protocol isn't trying to discover

> **"The correct number of LP Tokens."**

There isn't one.

Instead...

The protocol is trying to choose

> **The most useful accounting system.**

That is a completely different goal.
