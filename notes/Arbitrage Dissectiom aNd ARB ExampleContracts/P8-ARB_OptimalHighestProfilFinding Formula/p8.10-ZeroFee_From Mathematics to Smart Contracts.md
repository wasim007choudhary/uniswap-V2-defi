# The Mathematics Is Only Half The Story

Our derivation tells us

> **how much to trade**.

It does **not** execute the trade.

A smart contract still needs to:

1. Find an arbitrage opportunity.
2. Read both pools' reserves.
3. Compute the optimal trade size.
4. Execute both swaps.
5. Repay any flash loan or flash swap.
6. Keep the remaining tokens as profit.

The formula only solves **Step 3**.

# Real-World Considerations

Our derivation made several simplifying assumptions.

Real arbitrage bots also account for:

- Swap fees (0.30% on Uniswap V2).
- Flash loan or flash swap fees.
- Gas costs.
- Slippage during execution.
- MEV competition.
- Transaction ordering.
- Reverts if profit becomes negative before confirmation.

The mathematical optimum is only worthwhile if the **net profit** remains positive after accounting for these factors.
